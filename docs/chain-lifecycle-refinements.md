## 1. De-conflate Provenance (lineage) from Data Quality (completeness)

### 1.1 Context

The platform needs fused two distinct purposes, and the fusion had propagated
into the runtime envelope:

- `N-EDDT-002` (Orientation & Modelling) owned provenance, and `N-EDDT-008`
  (Data Completeness & Fidelity) re-asserted it. *Where-from* (provenance) and
  *how-complete* (quality) were entangled at the source.
- That produced `N-CL-003` ("lineage **and** known source gaps") and a runtime
  type in which `Gaps` (a completeness annotation) lived inside a `Provenance`
  entry (a lineage record).

Purpose → function → form: the fix corrects the needs first, narrows the chain
needs and requirements, and reshapes `Header` so its form mirrors the
now-distinct purposes — a **provenance axis** (lineage) and a **quality axis**
(completeness now; fidelity / confidence later), each independently extensible.
`Provenance` becomes pure lineage, forever gaps-free.

### 1.2 The two-axis model

| Axis       | Platform need         | Chain need(s)             | Header field        | Type                               |
|:-----------|:----------------------|:--------------------------|:--------------------|:-----------------------------------|
| Modelling  | N-EDDT-002 (reframed) | N-CL-001                  | identity / sequence | (unchanged Header fields)          |
| Provenance | N-EDDT-012 (new)      | N-CL-003 (narrowed)       | `Header.Provenance` | `Provenance []Origin`              |
| Quality    | N-EDDT-008 (reframed) | N-CL-002 + N-CL-007 (new) | `Header.Quality`    | `Quality { Gaps []SequenceRange }` |

### 1.5 Errata raised

`E-CL-005` (chain-lifecycle spec §6) records the de-conflation: `Gaps` relocated
from a `Provenance` entry to `Header.Quality`; source-attributed gaps removed
from `Provenance` (quality axis, deferred to the aggregator); `Provenance` is now
`[]Origin`.
